About Me

Researcher, engineer, and mentor at the Center for Applied AI.

I'm a PhD candidate in Computer Science at the University of Kentucky, where I also serve as the lead engineer and supervisor of machine learning research staff at the Center for Applied AI (CAAI). My work sits at the intersection of healthcare informatics, agentic AI systems, and applied machine learning — building tools that make research safer, faster, and more reproducible.

With 8+ peer-reviewed publications in venues like AMIA and Clinical Chemistry, I've contributed to projects ranging from automated research protocol adherence (SmartState) to secure medical AI agent orchestration using Model Context Protocols. I hold both a BS and MS in Computer Science from the University of Kentucky and have been recognized on the Dean's List six times.

Beyond research, I'm passionate about mentoring — I've advised 8+ undergraduate and intern students on projects spanning machine learning, IoT networking, and clinical applications. I'm an active member of the American Medical Informatics Association (AMIA) and regularly present at national conferences.
